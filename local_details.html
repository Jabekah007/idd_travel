Our Dedicated team will assist students with visa applications, ensuring they meet all necessary requirements for studying abroad.


Travel to Accra Zoo. Where you experience wildlife, you will see animals such as lion, the royal python, ostrich, camel, hyena among others.



Next stop is the Kwame Nkrumah Memorial park.Dedicated to the memory of the late Osagyefo Dr. Kwame Nkrumah, who is the first president and declarer of the independence of Ghana, the memorial park was built on a former British Polo field where Ghana’s independence was declared in 1957. The Mausoleum is a magnificent Marble Monument and the last resting place of the first President of Ghana and his wife, Fathia Nkrumah.


Visit the Art Centre or the Makola market. The Accra Art Center is a warren of stalls selling arts and crafts. There you would find what is virtually the tourist heaven of Ghana. It has everything from woven kente, cloth, wooden sculptures, bead works, leather bags, earings and other fashion accessories. The are a never ending array of good in many stalls that may you not even be able to shop.


The Makola Market on the other hand is a renowned market place and shopping center of the city of Accra, the capital of Ghana. It has a wide array of products sold in the market and its surrounding streets, from locally manufactured goods to imported goods. It has locally handcrafted beads for jewellery and accessory. There is nothing you can not get to buy from the Mokola market unless you do not no where it is sold in the market.


Our next stop is the Lagon Botanical Gardens, located in the University of Ghana, Accra. It is owned by the University of Ghana and managed by both the Department of Botany and Mulch Company Ltd. It has a natural vegetation of 50 hectares. It was founded in 1950. It is a recreational center with a playground, canopy walks, and natural outdoor space with lakes, these facilities enables the following activities Children's Playground High Rope Course, Junior Rope Course, Canoeing, Canopy Walk, The Woodlands, Bird Watching, Fishing, Cycling.

2 nights/3days Ghana-Western Region Tour.


Day 1


Pick up at Accra Mall for Ghanaian and The Kotoka International Airport for foreigners.


We shall travel straight to experience the Assin Manso Slave river. The Assin Manso Ancestral Slave River was one of the slave markets for gathering indigenes during the trans-Atlantic slave trade. Th River Site also served as the place where slaves had their last bath on African soil before being marched down to the slave castles of Elmina and Cape Coast along the coast.


After this experience, we shall drive to the Kakum National Park, located approximately one hour drive from cape coast, The Kakum National Park treasures many different plants and animal species and is very popular destination for tourists, hikers, botanists and birdwatcher from all around the world. It has canopy walks that you would experience too.

We would return to Cape Coast and pass the night.


Day2


Breakfast between 6:00am-7:00am


We shall then travel to experience the Elmina castle, where slaves are housed before their shipment to America in the era of slave trade.

Our next destination is the Nzulezu-stilt village tour after which we shall pass the night.


Day 3


We shall visit the tilt water, possibly do some donations to the community and buy souvenirs, engage with the community dwellers by listening to the wonderful stories of Nzulezo, take pictures and then depart to our pickup point in Accra.

5 nights/6days Ghana-Tamale Tour.


Day 1


Pickup is at the Kotoka International Airport for foreigners and Accra Mall for Ghanaian.


We shall begin our journey through Kwehu and experience the beautiful landscape of the region, we shall then continue to Kumasi and experience the Kejetia Market, the biggest market in West Africa, The market has a day care school for most children of the market women, also has hospital and bank. We shall then sleep over at a hotel at Kumasi.


Day2


The next morning, we shall have breakfast between 6:00am-7:00am. After which we shall visit the Manhyia palace and the Manhyia palace meusoleum, the palace of the Ashanti kings and the history of the Ashanti kingdom, some of their peculiar historical artifact and the “Golden stool” of the Ashanti kingdom can all be seen and experienced here.


We shall then pass through the Boabeng Fiema monkey sanctuary as we head to Tamale, the capital city of the Northern region of Ghana, on arrival, we shall tour the Tamale villages and experience how “Shear butter” is processed as well as how Batakari (smock) is woven, we would then retire to a hotel and pass the night.


Day 3


Breakfast between 6:00 am-7:00 am.


The Mole National Park is one of Ghana's seven national parks,it is the country's largest wildlife refuge.We will visit the Larabanga Mosque, the mosque , built in the Sudanese architectural style in the village of Larabanga, Ghana. It is the oldest mosque in the country and one of the oldest in West Africa, and has been referred to as the "Mecca of West Africa".


Our next destination is the Mystic-stone near the Larabanga mosque. Larabanga is a community known to be a site of myths and mystical happenings. The stone is known to return to its original place of rest after several attempts of moving it to a different location. We shall from here drive to Kintampo and pass the night.


Day 4


We shall have breakfast between 6:00 am-7:00 am after which we shall drive to the Kintampo water fall, one of Ghana' highest waterfalls located in Bono East region of Ghana.


We shall then drive to Back to Accra


Includes, Breakfast and dinner, transportation, accommodation for the two(3)nights and all site on the itinerary entrance fees.

End of Tour


2 night/ 3days Ghana-Volta Tour.


Day 1


Pick up at Accra Mall for Ghanaian and The Kotoka International Airport for foreigners.


We shall travel to the Shai Hills resource reserve where we shall experience nearly dozens of primary animal species including antelopes, bats, birds (such as violet Turaco, Paradise Flycatcher, Green Turaco, Red-billed Hornbill, Yellow-fronted Tinkerbird, and Red-necked Buzzard), baboons, cats, duiker, guinea fowls, kobs, green monkeys, monitor lizards, African python, royal python, and zebras. After the experience at Shai Hills, we shall drive to Akosombo and experience the “Dam City”. We shall go to the Electricity power generation station, Return to Alos Bay and engage in boat riding, swimming and other fun activities, we shall pass the night here at Alos Bay.


Day2


Breakfast between 6:00am-7:00 am. We shall then travel to the Volta region to experience the Amedzofe canopy walk and the Tafi Atome Monkey sanctuary. After the experiences at the canopy walk and the monkey sanctuary, we shall drive to Wli, to pass the night.


Day 3


Breakfast between 6:00 am-7:00 am.


We shall proceed to experience the Wli Waterfalls, the highest waterfall in Ghana and the tallest in West Africa. It has a lower and an upper fall. For hike lovers, The Mountain Afadja is an excellent location. Afadjato is the highest mountain in west Africa and its summit is 885 metres (2,904 ft) above sea level. After the hike, we shall drive back to Accra, the pick up point and this ends our tour.


Includes, Breakfast and dinner, transportation, accommodation for the two(2)nights and all site on the itinerary entrance fees.


End of Tour
